About

Allu Arjun, born on April 8, 1983, is an Indian film actor known for his work in the Telugu cinema. He comes from a film family background, with his father Allu Aravind being a renowned film producer. Allu Arjun made his acting debut in 2003 with the film "Gangotri," and since then, he has established himself as one of the leading actors in the Telugu film industry.

Career

Allu Arjun's career in the film industry spans over two decades. He has appeared in numerous successful films and has gained a massive fan following for his stylish on-screen presence and versatile performances. Some of his notable movies include "Arya," "Race Gurram," "Sarrainodu," and "Ala Vaikunthapurramloo." Allu Arjun has won several awards for his outstanding performances, including Filmfare Awards and Nandi Awards.

Awards

  • 2005: Filmfare Award for Best Actor - Telugu for the film "Arya"
  • 2011: Filmfare Award for Best Actor - Telugu for the film "Vedam"
  • 2015: Filmfare Award for Best Actor - Telugu for the film "Race Gurram"
  • 2009: Filmfare Award for Best Actor - Telugu for the film "Parugu"
  • 2016: Filmfare Award for Best Supporting Actor - Telugu for the film "Rrudhramadevi"
